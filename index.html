How Google Search and LinkedIn Search Differ – and Why You Should Use Both

Google Search on LinkedIn

  • Searches the public-facing parts of LinkedIn plus results from other sites.
  • Lets you use advanced Google operators (AND, OR, site:, intitle:, etc.) for precise searches.
  • Surfaces profiles and mentions outside LinkedIn (e.g. GitHub, portfolios, blogs).

Why use it

  • Free, no subscription limits.
  • Can reach people LinkedIn’s own search may not show.
  • Helps build a broader view of talent, not just within LinkedIn.
  • Flexible for creative sourcing.

LinkedIn’s Internal Search

  • Searches across the full LinkedIn database.
  • Offers built-in filters (location, company, industry, seniority, etc.).
  • Best when you need structured, platform-specific results.

Why use it

  • Access to the most complete LinkedIn profiles.
  • Fast and easy to narrow down with filters.
  • Designed for recruiter workflows.

Takeaway

  • **Google** is best for reach, flexibility, and going beyond LinkedIn.
  • **LinkedIn** is best for structured filters and depth inside the platform.
  • The strongest sourcing strategies use both together.

How to Use the Basic Search Fields

The tool generates a single, combined search string as you fill in the fields. You can test your query at any time by pressing the **Generate Query** button.

  • **Country-Specific LinkedIn Domain**: The search starts by selecting a LinkedIn domain. This is your foundation. The tool automatically adds the necessary `site:` operators to search only within that country's LinkedIn pages, **using only the current `/in` profile path.**
  • **Town/City (Optional)**: This field lets you narrow your search to a specific city or town. The tool will add this as an `AND` operator to your query.
  • **Okta Hub Locations**: You can select multiple Okta hub locations from this dropdown menu. The tool combines these locations with `OR` operators.
  • **Companies (inanchor: | OR)**: Enter a list of companies. Use the selector to choose between the **`inanchor:`** operator (targets profile links) or a standard **`OR`** text search.
  • **Job Titles (intitle: | OR)**: Enter job titles. Use the selector to choose between the **`intitle:`** operator (targets headlines) or a standard **`OR`** text search.
  • **Keywords & Phrases**: These sections let you add various keywords and skills to your search string. The tool automatically combines them using `OR` or `AND` as indicated by the labels.
  • **Exclusions (-)**: Use this section to exclude unwanted keywords from your search results. Each keyword will be added with a `-` (minus) operator.

Google Advanced Search Operators (General)

Below is a quick reference guide for building powerful Google search queries. For more details on these operators, check out this video: More on Boolean Operators

"search term"

Finds an exact match of a word or phrase. Example: `"digital marketing strategy"`

OR

Searches for either of two terms (inclusive). Example: `marketing OR advertising`

AND

Requires both terms to appear in the results. Example: `SEO AND analytics`

-

Excludes words or phrases from the results. Example: `apple -fruit`

*

A wildcard that matches any word or phrase. Example: `"best * tools"`

( )

Groups multiple operators or terms to control logic. Example: `(SEO OR PPC) AND analytics`

+

Forces the inclusion of a specific word, even if it’s a common word Google would normally ignore. Example: `linkedin +bengaluru`

define:

Shows Google’s dictionary definition. Example: `define:ontology`

filetype:

Limits results to a specific file format. Example: `marketing plan filetype:pdf`

site:

Limits results to a specific website or domain. Example: `site:linkedin.com "growth hacker"`

related:

Finds websites similar to a given site. Example: `related:hubspot.com`

intitle:

Finds pages with a specific word in the title. Example: `intitle:"case study"`

allintitle:

Finds pages with all specified words in the title. Example: `allintitle:SEO audit checklist`

inurl:

Finds pages with a word in the URL. Example: `inurl:resume "developer"`

allinurl:

Finds pages with all specified words in the URL. Example: `allinurl:research PDF`

intext:

Finds pages containing a specific word in the body text. Example: `intext:"customer journey"`

allintext:

Finds pages containing all specified words in the body text. Example: `allintext:content strategy SEO`

AROUND(X)

Finds words that are within X words of each other. Example: `"machine" AROUND(5) "learning"`
